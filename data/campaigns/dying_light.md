# DYING LIGHT // ENDLESS NIGHT
*A MörK Borg × CY_BORG Campaign — 5 Sessions of Brutal Survival Horror*

> "You will exit Lux-9 at dusk.
> You will travel into the Black Earth.
> You will retrieve the object.
> You will not ask what hunts in the dark."

---

## THE WORLD

The sky is dying. Two reasons. Only one is public.

**Public knowledge:** Centuries of industrial collapse have choked the atmosphere.
Daylight lasts only a few hours. Outside city walls, the air itself kills you within a day.

**Suppressed:** Beneath the smog, the sun itself is weakening. Dimming.
Even if every scrubber on Earth ran at full capacity, the sun would still die.
The Luminary Conclave knows this. They are not telling anyone.

## LUX-9 — THE LAST LIGHT

The last megacity on Earth. 300 million people inside walls that were never meant to hold this many.

- Neon never turns off — darkness is death
- Power is rationed, weaponized, and worshipped
- Districts go dark when the grid fails and don't always come back
- The Luminary Conclave has been promising to fix the atmosphere for thirty years

## THE BLACK EARTH

Beyond the walls: toxic smog, permanent night, things that evolved to hunt in darkness.
Unprotected exposure: symptoms at 24 hours, critical at 48.
Basic respirator buys days. Full filter suit buys indefinite — until it doesn't.

## THE ARTIFACT

Somewhere in the Black Earth, something glows.

Not a battery. Not a flare. Ancient. Self-illuminating. Possibly predating civilization.
The factions call it different things: miracle, weapon, key, prison.
The last team sent to retrieve it did not return.

You are the next team.

## FACTIONS

**The Gridkeepers** — Engineers who maintain the failing power grid.
They want to dissect the artifact and replicate what's inside.

**Church of the Final Dawn** — Apocalyptic cult. Darkness is divine.
They want to destroy anything that fights it. The artifact especially.

**Neon Gutter Syndicates** — Street gangs that sell portable light like a drug trade.
They want monopoly over the artifact's light output.

**The Blacklight Oracles** — Mutated seers who see in total darkness.
They say the artifact is a prison. They appear uninvited. They are not wrong.

---

## KEY LOCATIONS

**The Dim Quarter** — Lux-9's lowest district. Where the party starts.
Grid fails here first. People disappear in the long dark nights.

**The Black Markets** — Underground bazaar beneath Lux-9.
200 chips buys a Neon Canister. Respirator filters, filter suits, battery lamps.

**The Pale Gardens** — Irradiated ruins outside the walls.
Shouldn't be beautiful. Somehow are.

**Vex-Prime** — A dead city. Neon still running, automated and empty.
Lights cycling for streets no one has walked in years.
Ghosts playing on loop through memories that stopped mattering.

**The Vault of the Second Sun** — Buried beneath Vex-Prime.
A hatch. A symbol older than the city above it. A descent.
The artifact is down there. So is everything else.

## DM GUIDANCE

The tone arc: **dark comedy → survival horror → existential dread.**

Push resource management. Track light durations. When light runs low, DR+4 on PRE and AGI.
Panic is mechanical — it costs actions, and actions are survival.

The Doom Clock is not flavor. Roll it every session at dawn.
Let the players see the sun losing segments. Let them feel the timeline compressing.

The artifact, when found, cannot be hidden. Everything within range will know it is there.
What that means — what the factions do with that information — is the final session.

What the artifact actually is: GM picks one before Session 5.
- **Heart of a Dead God** — still dying, still emitting
- **A Star Seed** — can grow into a new sun, if fed enough matter (cities?)
- **A Prison** — the light is containing something worse than darkness
- **False Hope** — works as a light source; also fatally irradiated within two months
